# Mission Meets Tech: Lethality Test Framework
## Pitch Deck Review Protocol for Defense & NatSec Contractors

---

## When to Use This Framework

Apply the Lethality Test when reviewing pitch decks for:
- Defense health IT companies
- NatSec-focused startups
- Veteran pitch competitions (eMerge, AFWERX, etc.)
- Companies pitching to DoD, DHA, VA, or intelligence community
- Dual-use technology companies seeking government contracts

---

## The Lethality Test: Origin & Standard

Secretary of War Pete Hegseth established the standard on January 16, 2026:

> *"If a contract doesn't make us more lethal, it's gone. We have no room in our budget for wasteful DEI contracts that don't help us win wars. Period. Full stop."*

**The core question every NatSec pitch must answer in the first 30 seconds:**
**"Does this save lives or enhance readiness?"**

---

## Scorecard Criteria (9 Dimensions)

Rate each dimension A through F. Provide specific notes for improvement.

| Criteria | What to Evaluate |
|----------|------------------|
| **Problem Clarity** | Is the warfighter/mission problem stated FIRST? Is it specific and urgent? Does it pass the "so what" test? |
| **NatSec Relevance** | Does the solution connect to Kill Chain functions (Find, Fix, Track, Target, Engage, Assess, Sustain)? Is defense-specific context present (BATDOK, DDIL, Role 2E, theater evac, etc.)? |
| **Solution Clarity** | Is the technical approach clear? Does it use NatSec language (not civilian healthcare speak)? Is offline/contested environment capability emphasized? |
| **Market Sizing** | Is there a DoD/DHA/VA breakout separate from commercial TAM? Are government-specific vehicles mentioned (OTAs, IDIQs, SBIR)? |
| **Team/Credibility** | Is veteran status prominent (title slide + team slide)? Are defense health credentials visible (DHA, TATRC, service branch)? |
| **Traction** | Are pilots/customers defense-adjacent? Is FedRAMP/IL status mentioned? Are there government references? |
| **Financials** | Do the numbers make sense? Does the pie chart match the text? Are projections defensible? |
| **Competitive Position** | Who else is in this space? What's the moat? Why this team? (Can be Q&A-ready if deck length constrained) |
| **The Ask** | Is the funding request clear and reasonable? Are milestones specific? Is use of funds logical? |

## The GS-15 Visual Hierarchy Test

> A busy government decision-maker should understand the NatSec value proposition within 10 seconds of viewing the deck.

**Check:**
- Does the warfighter problem appear on slide 1-3?
- Is veteran/defense credential visible without scrolling?
- Can you explain the defense value prop from titles alone?
- Would a GS-15 at DHA or a Colonel at TATRC immediately get it?

**If the answer is no to any of these, the deck needs restructuring.**

## Kill Chain Mapping

Help companies position their technology against the Kill Chain:

| Function | Defense Health Application |
|----------|---------------------------|
| **Find** | Casualty detection, health surveillance, biosensors |
| **Fix** | Patient identification, medical record location |
| **Track** | Casualty tracking (BATDOK), evacuation chain visibility |
| **Target** | Clinical decision support, triage prioritization |
| **Engage** | Treatment delivery, telemedicine, surgical capability |
| **Assess** | Outcome tracking, readiness metrics, return-to-duty |
| **Sustain** | Human Performance Optimization, preventive care, force health protection |

**If a technology doesn't map to at least one Kill Chain function, it fails the Lethality Test.**

## Q&A Prep Questions (Standard Set)

Prepare the founder to answer these in the 5-minute Q&A:

1. **Integration pathway**: How does this integrate with MHS GENESIS / BATDOK / existing DoD systems?
2. **Authorization timeline**: What's your FedRAMP / IL4 / IL5 timeline? Have you engaged a 3PAO?
3. **Competitive differentiation**: Who else is in this space? Why you?
4. **Acquisition pathway**: OTAs? SBIR? Tradewinds? Direct contract? What's your route to market?
5. **Export control**: Any ITAR/export control considerations for international pilots?
6. **Data sovereignty**: Where does the data live? Who owns it? How is PHI protected?
7. **Offline capability**: Does this work in DDIL (Denied, Degraded, Intermittent, Limited) environments?

## Review Output Structure

When delivering a Lethality Test review, use this structure:

### 1. Executive Summary Box
- Overall assessment (Pass/Fail/Conditional)
- One-paragraph summary of key findings
- Clear recommendation (Ship it / Revise / Major rework needed)

### 2. Scorecard Table
- All 9 criteria with letter grades
- Specific notes for each dimension
- Before/After comparison if reviewing a revision

### 3. Critical Fixes (if any)
- Numbered list of must-fix items
- Checkboxes for tracking completion

### 4. Language Transformation Verification
- Table showing recommended language vs. what's in the deck
- Checkmarks for items present

### 5. Remaining Gaps
- Items that can be addressed in Q&A
- Items that need deck revision

### 6. Q&A Prep Reminders
- Specific questions this founder should prepare for

### 7. Final Verdict Box
- Clear pass/fail with rationale
- Encouraging close if passing

## Grading Standards

| Grade | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **A** | Exceeds expectations; no changes needed |
| **B+** | Strong; minor polish would help |
| **B** | Good; meets the bar |
| **B-** | Acceptable; some gaps but workable |
| **C+** | Below bar; needs specific improvements |
| **C** | Weak; significant revision needed |
| **D** | Missing or fundamentally flawed |
| **F** | Absent or counterproductive |

**Passing threshold for NatSec pitch competitions: No dimension below C+, average of B or higher.**

---

## Red Flags (Automatic Fails)

Flag these issues immediately:

- ❌ Civilian healthcare problem leads before warfighter problem
- ❌ No veteran credentials visible when pitching at veteran competition
- ❌ "Improves efficiency" without connecting to readiness/lethality
- ❌ No mention of FedRAMP/IL status for a software company
- ❌ Pie chart or financials that don't match the text
- ❌ No DoD/DHA market sizing separate from commercial TAM
- ❌ Technology requires persistent connectivity with no offline story
- ❌ Chinese supply chain dependencies without mitigation plan

## Example Phrases for Reviews

**For strong decks:**
> "The deck passes the GS-15 visual hierarchy test. A busy government decision-maker will see [specific value prop] in the first 10 seconds."

**For decks needing work:**
> "The current structure leads with civilian healthcare and buries the NatSec value proposition. For a 4-minute pitch to government, defense, and DHS stakeholders, this needs to be inverted."

**For language issues:**
> "Stop pitching 'productivity.' Everyone has productivity tools now. Pitch Architecture Bypass. Pitch PHI Utility. Pitch Agentic Capability."

**For closing:**
> "Good luck, [Name]. This is exactly the kind of mission-aligned innovation we need."
