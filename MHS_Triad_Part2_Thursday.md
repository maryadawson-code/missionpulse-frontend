## H2F: The Proof of Concept

The Army's **Holistic Health and Fitness (H2F)** program is Via's doctrine made real. Now deployed to 71 brigades (up from 50 in October 2024) with full Army implementation targeted by 2032 (236 brigades). The numbers are striking:

- **35% reduction** in injury volume
- **37% fewer suicides** versus 37% *increase* in non-H2F units
- **23% higher** ACFT pass rate
- **27% more** soldiers qualifying expert in rifle marksmanship

Each H2F Performance Team includes 20-22 contractors: strength coaches, dietitians, physical therapists, cognitive performance specialists. Cost is $3 million per brigade to field, $2.5 million annual sustainment. The Army's Warrior Performance Platform just got DoD IL4 authorization: 2,300 soldiers registered, 70,000 workout sets tracked, 150,000 wearable data points captured.

**The Gap:** Here's the problem for Via: outside special operations, neither the Navy nor Air Force has anything comparable to H2F. The Navy's NOFFS provides resources but no embedded teams. GAO found only 14% of Navy officers get adequate sleep. The Air Force's Comprehensive Airman Fitness is a framework, not a program.

The exception? The **Space Force**, which deployed Guardian Resilience Teams to all bases with certified strength coaches, mental health providers, and physical therapists. Their September 2025 Human Performance and Readiness Manual is the closest service equivalent to Army H2F.

Here's the legislative hook that makes expansion possible: the FY2026 NDAA includes **"SPEED Act" language** mandating portfolio-based acquisition. This allows Via to buy "Human Performance" as a **portfolio capability** rather than running 50 separate contracts for dietitians, wearables, and gyms. That legal change is what enables the H2F expansion we're predicting.

## The Simulation Layer: Why SANTOS Matters

H2F costs $3 million per brigade to field. Via can't afford to scale that investment across the joint force and then watch soldiers break anyway. He needs a way to validate load configurations, predict injury risk, and prove ROI before the money moves.

That's where **SANTOS** comes in. Developed by the University of Iowa's Virtual Soldier Research program with $60 million in military R&D investment, SANTOS is already operational with the Marines through **GruntSim**. The platform uses physics-driven human modeling (215 degrees of freedom, integrated biomechanics and physiology) to predict body stress, fatigue, hydration requirements, and injury risk under specific load configurations, all before live execution.

The product suite includes GruntSim (load-bearing simulation for Marines), iPredict (AI-driven injury prediction built under an ONR contract), and EKTIMO (motion capture to biomechanics). The Marines are already using it. The question is whether DHA, under Via's leadership, recognizes that Human Performance Optimization requires simulation, not just monitoring.

The closed loop: wearables collect real-time biometrics; SANTOS predicts what happens next. H2F proves embedded teams reduce injuries by 35%. SANTOS tells those teams which soldiers are at risk before the injury happens. That's risk mitigation for the Human Weapon System investment.

## The Telehealth Landscape: What Matters

**The Big Three:**

**Amwell/Leidos** ($180M contract) is the enterprise flagship, integrated with MHS GENESIS. Virtual visits nearly tripled since deployment, with expansion to combat zones, the Coast Guard, and Military Entrance Processing Command. But the August 2025 extension cut behavioral health and remote care programs due to budget restrictions. SEC filings acknowledge they may not maintain the DHA relationship beyond July 2026 under comparable terms.

**GlobalMed** holds the Med COI Authority to Operate across all 721 MTFs, making them the only synchronous clinical telehealth platform with that authorization. Their hardware-integrated approach (40+ medical devices, transportable exam stations) enables clinical-grade remote examinations. They power telehealth for the White House Medical Unit, have been in the VA since 2008, and recently hired Major General Michael Talley as Senior Advisor. Bass spent 15 months there as SVP. They know MTF operations cold.

**Medweb** ($52.8M) owns the deployed mission: PACS and tele-radiology on 40+ Navy vessels and all six Combatant Commands. Their DICOM data scrubber handles security challenges of moving medical imaging across classification levels, which is critical for Via's Role 2E vision.

**The one to watch:** **Outpatient App** just secured their SBIR Phase III, positioning them for the Tradewinds marketplace. They solve real MTF problems (medical readiness coordination, deployment clearance workflows that currently require searching five databases and two networks) with Air Force backing. That's the combination that gets you from pilot to production.

**What's Missing:**

**Remote Patient Monitoring** represents the biggest gap. DHA conducted a Reverse Industry Day in December 2022 soliciting commercial RPM capabilities. No enterprise contract has been awarded. The program remains in market research.

**The Oura wearables deal is dead.** The $96M sole-source contract collapsed after WHOOP filed two GAO protests. But the protest was just the trigger. The real story? DHA realized it couldn't audit the data. The next wearables solicitation won't ask for a ring or a strap. It will ask for **Audit-Ready Data Architecture**.

**Behavioral health digital therapeutics** took a hit when the Amwell extension excluded these programs. No enterprise platform comparable to Lyra Health or Headspace is deployed.

**Oracle's Clinical AI Agent** is already here and scaling through an active DHMSM task order. Ambient listening that generates structured notes directly into MHS GENESIS. Clinicians are seeing 2+ hours per day returned to patient care. If you're in ambient documentation, you missed the boat.

**The Digital Front Door is dead.** December 2024 prototype awards to BDR, Bluestaq, Clearstep, and Ernst & Young will not move forward to production. Budget pressures killed it.

## What This Means for You

**For Contractors:**

The three magic words are **governance**, **speed**, and **survivability**. Bass needs AI that audits itself for compliance. Michael needs solutions that deploy into the GenAI.mil and AskSage ecosystems by April. Via needs systems that work when the network goes dark.

> **The three magic words are governance, speed, and survivability.**

If you're in operational telehealth, understand the full landscape. GlobalMed has the ATO and the MTF footprint; Medweb has the deployed ships and the DICOM expertise; Amwell has the enterprise virtual visit platform but faces contract uncertainty beyond July 2026. Position for "yes, and" contract structures rather than winner-take-all competitions.

If you're building data platforms, RPM solutions, or wearables: stop marketing to "beneficiary wellness" and start speaking the language of Human Performance Optimization. Your value proposition isn't reducing hospitalizations for retirees. It's maintaining combat power through continuous health monitoring. Same technology, completely different buyer psychology.

**Learn from the Oura disaster.** The $96M sole-source contract didn't die because WHOOP filed a protest. It died because DHA couldn't audit the data. The next wearables solicitation will require **Audit-Ready Data Architecture** as table stakes. If you're pitching wearables to DHA, you need three things: (1) a form-factor-agnostic value proposition that survives competitive procurement, (2) clear documentation showing how your data flows into MHS GENESIS with full audit traceability, and (3) a domestic manufacturing story that satisfies supply chain security concerns.

**Outpatient App shows how it's done.** They didn't try to boil the ocean with an enterprise platform pitch. They solved specific MTF problems (medical readiness coordination, provider-to-provider communication, deployment clearance workflows), built relationships with service leadership, and worked the SBIR pathway from Phase I to Phase III. Now they're positioned for Tradewinds. That's the playbook: solve real problems, get service backing, use the innovation pathways, then scale through marketplace mechanisms.

**The critical gaps are opportunities:** Enterprise RPM has been in market research since 2022. Someone's going to win that contract. Behavioral health digital therapeutics got cut from the Amwell extension. The need hasn't gone away.

**For Beneficiaries:**

The telehealth push is coming, whether you like it or not. If you're a retiree or dependent, expect your care to move increasingly to virtual platforms. The MTF down the road may look very different in three years.

**For the Warfighter:**

This is actually good news. The MHS is finally getting serious about combat medicine. The investment in Role 2E, in autonomous logistics, in Human Performance Optimization: this is what a military health system focused on lethality looks like.

## Predictions: 2026 and Beyond

Here's where we put some stakes in the ground.

**2026:**

Via confirmed as DHA Director by March. Bass announces a "Virtual Care First" directive by summer, setting targets for shifting routine beneficiary care off MTF campuses. The MHS GENESIS cloud migration hits turbulence (expect at least one major delay announcement). GenAI.mil and AskSage adoption metrics become leadership performance indicators. Oracle's Clinical AI Agent scales beyond initial deployment sites; watch for 30%+ MTF coverage by year end.

At least one major university medical center announces it's withdrawing from DoD research partnerships over the indirect cost fight. DHA issues an RFI on "Human Performance Optimization data architecture," the first signal they're serious about the RPM/wearables foundation.

The Amwell contract uncertainty resolves: either DHA extends through 2027 or begins transition planning to a successor platform.

**2027-2028:**

The first Role 2E unit achieves initial operating capability in the Pacific. DHA consolidates telehealth platforms into a tiered structure: Amwell (or successor) for high-volume beneficiary virtual visits, GlobalMed for clinical-grade MTF telehealth, Medweb for deployed operational environments. Whoever can demonstrate seamless data handoff across tiers wins.

Wearables get a second chance, but only with audit-ready architecture. DHA reissues a wearables solicitation with form-factor-agnostic requirements and a data governance framework that satisfies auditors. The winner (likely a competitive award this time) begins deployment to conventional infantry units beyond SOF.

The H2F outcome gap becomes politically untenable: Army units show 35% fewer injuries and 37% fewer suicides while Navy and Air Force conventional forces lack comparable programs. Congress mandates joint implementation.

The convergence happens: continuous physiological monitoring feeds AI decision support (GenAI.mil/AskSage processing clinical data), which informs autonomous trauma systems (DARPA MASH/GOLDEVAC transitioning to operational units). The "Human Weapon System" doctrine has the technology stack to back it up, if the transition pathways from DARPA to DHA acquisition don't get stuck in the valley of death.

Either the Triad's bet pays off with a military health system ready for great power conflict, or the beneficiary backlash forces a course correction.

## The Question That Matters

Here's what we keep coming back to: **Does this save lives or enhance readiness?**

The Triad is betting that by stripping the MHS of its corporate, benefit-centric identity, they can build something that actually performs in the worst-case scenario: a great power war in the Pacific.

They might be right. The jalopy we've been driving for 30 years wasn't going to make it.

But transitions are messy. The academic research community is already at war with Michael. The beneficiary population is going to push back on Bass's privatization drive. And Via's operational concepts depend on technology that doesn't exist yet.

The next three years are going to be wild.
