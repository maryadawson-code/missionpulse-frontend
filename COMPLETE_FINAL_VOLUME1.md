The Defense Health Agency stands at a critical juncture. The transition from fragmented, siloed data environments to a mission-enabling strategic asset—the "VAULTIS" vision—requires more than technical implementation. It demands an Independent Governance Partner capable of providing neutral oversight between Agency mission owners and incumbent engineering vendors.

rockITdata offers that independence. As a services-only contractor with no ownership stake in proprietary data fabrics, hosting contracts, or catalog platforms, rockITdata possesses no organizational conflict of interest. Our role is singular: audit, document, and govern DHA's data assets to ensure they serve operational requirements through a unified governance framework. Crucially, while rockITdata facilitates the governance process, the Government retains all final authority. rockITdata does not approve data for enterprise use, authorize AI consumption, or select the final Enterprise Data Catalog.

**[Figure 1: Governance-First Approach Through Neutral Oversight and Pre-Ingestion Data Quality]**

Central to our approach is resolution of a critical challenge in federal data management—the mistaken belief that procuring a new data platform will inherently remediate data quality issues. Evidence consistently demonstrates that platform acquisition without prior governance establishment results in accelerated propagation of existing data inefficiencies. Organizations invest millions in modern data infrastructure only to discover they have amplified problems rather than resolved them.

This proposal resolves that challenge for DHA. Rather than relying on unproven technical platforms, rockITdata proposes a Human-Centric Governance Framework supported by automation that facilitates systemic remediation of data quality prior to ingestion into the enterprise fabric. Within the first 90 days, this approach reduces Government risk by establishing a validated data inventory and "Approval Gate" prototype, preventing immediate ingestion of non-compliant or poor-quality data. This ensures strict compliance with DHA PGI 224.1-90, providing proactive controls for Personally Identifiable Information and Protected Health Information.

## SECTION 2.0 OBJECTIVE 1: COMPREHENSIVE BASELINE DATA INVENTORY

**SOO Requirement 5.2.I.A:** Systematically document and categorize up to 80 DHA authoritative and secondary-use data systems including detailed metadata, and establish collaboration framework with Data Owners, Stewards, and SMEs.

### 2.1 Technical Approach

The challenge before DHA is substantial: transform fragmented knowledge of 80 diverse systems—spanning decades of technology evolution from legacy mainframes to cloud-native platforms—into a comprehensive, validated enterprise data census. rockITdata addresses this challenge through a Tiered Metadata Ingestion Methodology that balances coverage velocity with governance rigor.

Not all systems warrant identical treatment. Mission-critical clinical platforms like MHS GENESIS contain complex schemas demanding intensive engagement with subject matter experts. Archival systems require thorough documentation but benefit from automated harvesting rather than manual deep-dives. Standard platforms with well-documented schemas can be efficiently crawled with minimal human intervention. Our tiered approach optimizes resource allocation accordingly.

**[Figure 2: Tiered Inventory Methodology showing risk-based system prioritization and engagement strategies progressing from Tier 1 deep-dives through Tier 3 automated crawling]**

Tier 1 systems—including MHS GENESIS, DML-ES, and DMACS—receive high-touch treatment through structured in-person sessions at DHHQ during Months 1-4. These deep-dives employ offline metadata ingestion, protecting production operations while capturing comprehensive schema documentation. Tier 2 archival systems undergo header-only automated harvesting during Months 4-7, reducing effort for stable, well-understood platforms. Tier 3 standard systems receive full automated crawling via Python scripts during Months 5-9, maximizing efficiency where system architectures permit.

Every metadata record, regardless of tier, progresses through our 4-Phase Metadata Lifecycle ensuring Government-controlled quality gates. Records enter as Drafts with required field validation, advance through Governance Lead validation for accuracy and standards compliance, require explicit Data Owner approval with CAC-attributed authorization, and achieve Publication status only after complete human chain-of-custody verification. Government may pause any record at any phase—automation accelerates work but never bypasses human authority.

**[Figure 3: 4-Phase Metadata Lifecycle Workflow depicting linear progression from Draft through Publication with Government control gates]**

**AMANDA™ Application to Objective 1:**
- **Automated:** Python/SQL harvesting pipelines extract technical metadata without manual intervention
- **Managed:** Every system maps to identified Owner/Steward/Custodian creating clear accountability
- **Accountable:** CAC-attributed approvals at each lifecycle phase ensure traceability
- **Neutral:** Standards-based schema (DCAT, ISO 11179) avoids vendor-specific formats
- **Decisive:** Escalation procedures prevent indefinite validation delays
- **Auditable:** Complete 4-Phase lifecycle documentation supports compliance verification

Our tooling leverages Government-provided capabilities exclusively: Python, SQL, and R for extraction and analysis; SharePoint Online GCC-H for collaboration; Power Apps for intake forms; AskSage and AWS Bedrock for AI-assisted documentation with mandatory Human-in-the-Loop validation. All work aligns with DoD Data Strategy VAULTIS principles, NIST SP 800-53, ISO 11179, and DHA PGI 224.1-90.

### 2.2 Execution Timeline and Integration

The inventory executes across four major phases. During Months 1-2, we develop metadata schemas aligned with DoD standards, establish tier classification criteria for Government approval, and identify Data Owners and Stewards for all 80 systems. Months 2-4 focus on Tier 1 execution through intensive DHHQ sessions, with SME validation occurring concurrently. Months 4-9 expand coverage through automated harvesting of Tier 2 and Tier 3 systems, with Owner approval workflows processing validated records continuously. Final validation and DHA-EDC migration mapping complete during Months 9-12.

This inventory does not exist in isolation. Completed records populate the Interim Repository immediately, informing AoA evaluation criteria with real operational requirements. Automation pipelines accelerate Tier 2/3 completion while maintaining quality. Federation capabilities export approved metadata to Advana for OSD visibility. UAT sessions use inventory data for realistic discovery scenarios.

### 2.3 Challenges and Mitigations

System access constraints represent the most significant risk to comprehensive coverage. Security policies, operational restrictions, or vendor licensing may limit automated extraction from sensitive environments. rockITdata coordinates with DHA stakeholders to obtain required access in accordance with DHA processes and schedules. Our mitigation employs multiple pathways: direct SQL queries where authorized, vendor API integration for proprietary systems, structured SME interviews for restricted environments, and standardized manual templates as fallback. These layered approaches ensure comprehensive coverage regardless of access constraints.

Stakeholder availability presents scheduling challenges given operational demands on clinical and administrative personnel. Flexible scheduling with multiple session options, virtual participation eliminating travel requirements, and asynchronous validation enabling completion at stakeholder convenience address these constraints. Escalation procedures to the Governance Council maintain program momentum.

Metadata inconsistency across 80 systems accumulated over decades creates semantic alignment challenges. Our standardized schema enforces consistent field population, validation checkpoints identify deviations requiring remediation, and iterative refinement with domain owners resolves ambiguities before publication.

### 2.4 Expected Outcomes and Value

Upon completion, DHA will possess authoritative documentation of all 80 systems with high metadata field completion rates—a comprehensive enterprise data census enabling informed decision-making. Time-to-discover relevant data products will decrease significantly, from weeks of tribal knowledge consultation to hours of catalog search. Every published record will carry documented Owner approval, creating audit-ready accountability supporting compliance verification. Most importantly, this inventory establishes the foundation for all subsequent governance capabilities: federation, lifecycle management, and enterprise catalog migration.

**Deliverables:** Comprehensive Baseline Inventory (JSON/CSV); Metadata Quality Reports (monthly); Governance Approval Records; DHA-EDC Ingest Mapping; Standards Documentation.

## SECTION 3.0 OBJECTIVE 2: INTERIM CENTRALIZED REPOSITORY

**SOO Requirement 5.2.I.B:** Develop interim centralized repository for baseline inventory and documentation with secure, accessible storage serving as foundational knowledge management until DHA-EDC establishment.

### 3.1 Technical Approach

Commercial data catalog procurement typically requires 6-12 months for vendor selection, 3-6 months for ATO processing, and additional months for implementation—timeline incompatible with DHA's immediate governance needs. rockITdata's solution eliminates this delay through a Zero-Footprint Governance Proving Ground deployed entirely on existing DHA infrastructure.

**[Figure 4: Zero-Footprint Architecture depicting SharePoint GCC-H foundation with Power Platform integration layer]**

The repository leverages SharePoint Online GCC-H, Power Apps, Power Automate, and Power BI—platforms already authorized within DHA's environment. This approach provides significant advantages:

**Accelerated Time-to-Capability:** Deploying on authorized platforms enables governance operations within 8 weeks, compared to 12-18 months typically associated with new platform procurement and accreditation.

**Resource Efficiency:** Utilizing existing GFE avoids annual licensing costs associated with commercial catalogs during the interim period while DHA evaluates enterprise catalog options.

**Reduced Accreditation Risk:** Operating within already-authorized environments supports immediate compliance without requiring new Authority to Operate processes.

The architecture implements a 6-Zone Information Model providing comprehensive governance capability. Zone 1 (Inventory) maintains the operational catalog with normalized relationships connecting Systems to Datasets to Data Elements. Zone 2 (Standards) enforces consistency through schemas, controlled vocabularies, and validation rules. Zone 3 (Governance) tracks accountability with Owner/Steward rosters and approval workflows. Zone 4 (Lineage) visualizes data flows from source systems through transformation to consumption points. Zone 5 (Compliance) preserves audit evidence including privacy classifications, access logs, and retention schedules. Zone 6 (AI Reasoning) provides transparency for AI-assisted documentation through model pedigree tracking and confidence scores.

**[Figure 5: 6-Zone Information Model illustrating normalized repository architecture]**

**AMANDA™ Application to Objective 2:**
- **Automated:** Power Automate workflows handle approval routing, notifications, and lifecycle transitions
- **Managed:** 6-Zone architecture creates clear organizational structure for all governance artifacts
- **Accountable:** RBAC via SharePoint Groups with CAC-attributed actions ensures personnel traceability
- **Neutral:** Open standards (DCAT, ISO 11179) support any future catalog selection
- **Decisive:** Defined review cycles maintain program momentum
- **Auditable:** Glass Box dashboard provides real-time visibility into all program activities

Security implements defense-in-depth through Role-Based Access Control via SharePoint Groups, separation between edit and publish permissions preventing unauthorized release, metadata-only storage excluding actual PII/PHI content, complete CAC-attributed audit trails, and Zero Trust authentication patterns.

### 3.2 Execution Timeline

Retrieval-Augmented Generation processes metadata in-context without training models on DHA data, preventing data leakage or contamination. Confidence scoring routes outputs appropriately: high-confidence items proceed to streamlined steward review, while low-confidence items require specialized SME attention. Model pedigree tracking documents complete AI lineage enabling bias identification and error tracing.

**[Figure 8: Human-in-the-Loop Validation Workflow ensuring no AI output reaches authoritative state without human approval]**

Data Owners provide final approval, advancing status to Authoritative. Only after complete human validation does metadata publish for enterprise consumption.

## OPTIONAL CLIN: OBJECTIVE 9 — DATA PRODUCT LIFECYCLE GOVERNANCE

**SOO Requirement 5.2.I.I:** Establish governance model for end-to-end Data Product lifecycle including creation standards, quality management, versioning controls, publication/retirement procedures, and consumer feedback mechanisms.

### 9.1 Strategic Approach

Data without lifecycle governance accumulates indefinitely, quality degrades invisibly, and consumers lose confidence in catalog reliability. rockITdata treats data as managed products with defined creation, operation, and retirement phases—analogous to software product management. This shifts DHA from reactive data administration to proactive data product stewardship.

**[Figure 16: Data Product Lifecycle Model showing six stages with quality gates and governance checkpoints]**

The lifecycle philosophy recognizes that data products have finite useful lives—not infinite persistence. Quality gates prevent substandard products from reaching consumers. Versioning enables controlled evolution without breaking dependent systems. Retirement is planned and documented, not accidental deletion creating audit gaps.

Objective 9 builds on the federated governance foundation established in Objective 8, applying lifecycle discipline to data products managed through federated stewardship structures.

### 9.2 Data Product Creation Standards

Every data product entering the catalog requires consistent definition. Identity components include unique catalog identifier and standardized naming following style guide conventions. Ownership components identify accountable Data Owner, assigned Steward, and Technical Custodian. Metadata components encompass schema documentation, business definition, lineage mapping, and quality score. Quality components specify fitness criteria including completeness, accuracy, and timeliness thresholds. Access components document classification level, authorized consumer groups, and access request workflows.

Creation templates standardize onboarding: Product Registration Forms capture identity, ownership, initial metadata, and quality targets; Quality Certification Checklists document required gate passage and reviewer attestation; Consumer Agreements establish terms of use, refresh expectations, and feedback channels.

### 9.3 Quality Management

**[Figure 17: Quality Gate Framework showing validation checkpoints with pass/fail criteria]**

Quality dimensions include completeness (required fields populated), accuracy (values matching authoritative sources), timeliness (data freshness meeting product-specific SLAs), and consistency (cross-system alignment).

Three quality gates prevent substandard publication. Gate 1 (Draft Quality) validates required fields and format compliance before steward review. Gate 2 (Steward Review) validates business accuracy and lineage verification before approval. Gate 3 (Publication Ready) confirms all thresholds met and Owner approved.

Quality dashboards provide visibility into product quality scores enabling proactive remediation.

### 9.4 Versioning and Change Control

Schema evolution requires disciplined versioning preventing consumer disruption. Major versions indicate breaking schema changes requiring consumer migration—these require Change Control Board approval and advance notice. Minor versions indicate additive changes maintaining backward compatibility—these require Steward approval with consumer notification. Patch versions indicate quality fixes with no consumer impact—these proceed under Steward authority.

The Change Control Board comprises Governance Lead, Domain Stewards, and Technical Representatives meeting bi-weekly. The Board approves or rejects major changes, sets deprecation timelines, and resolves versioning disputes.

### 9.5 Publication and Retirement

Publication workflow ensures governance completion before consumer access. Products proceed through creation, quality gates, steward validation, owner approval, publication, and consumer access.

**[Figure 18: Controlled Retirement Procedure showing four phases]**

Retirement proceeds through four controlled phases: Deprecation Notice alerts consumers and provides migration guidance; Read-Only halts updates while allowing continued consumption; Archive preserves metadata for audit continuity; Tombstone creates permanent record with redirect to successor.

### 9.6 Consumer Feedback and Continuous Improvement

Feedback mechanisms capture consumer experience across multiple channels: in-catalog ratings, issue submissions, enhancement requests, and satisfaction surveys.

Product Health Scores combine quality metrics, consumer ratings, and usage trends into composite indicators enabling proactive product management.
