## SECTION 5: FAR 52.219-14 COMPLIANCE FRAMEWORK [NEW]

### 5.1 Compliance Thresholds by Set-Aside Type

| Set-Aside Type | Minimum Prime + SB % | Maximum Large Sub % |
|----------------|---------------------|---------------------|
| WOSB Set-Aside | ≥51% | ≤49% |
| SDVOSB Set-Aside | ≥51% | ≤49% |
| 8(a) Competitive | ≥50% | ≤50% |
| HUBZone | ≥50% | ≤50% |
| VA SDVOSB (VAAR 852.219-74) | ≥50% | ≤50% |

**Target Buffer:** Maintain large sub at ≤40% to provide compliance margin.

### 5.2 Cost of Personnel Calculation Method

```
Cost of Personnel = Direct Labor + Fringe Benefits
(Excludes: Overhead, G&A, Fee, ODCs, Materials, Travel)

Compliance Ratio = (Prime + Small Business Cost of Personnel) / (Total Cost of Personnel)
```

**Agent must calculate and validate Cost of Personnel ratio before finalizing any staffing plan.**

### 5.3 Compliance Validation Checklist

Before submitting any labor mix:
- [ ] Calculate total Cost of Personnel (Prime + Small Subs + Large Subs)
- [ ] Verify Prime + Small Sub % meets threshold for set-aside type
- [ ] Confirm Large Sub % is below regulatory maximum
- [ ] Flag any period where compliance is at risk
- [ ] Document assumptions and escalate if near threshold

## SECTION 6: TEAMING DESIGN RULES

### 6.1 Large Business Subcontractors
- Use for legacy expertise, incumbency knowledge, or proprietary systems
- Leverage for past performance credibility
- **Actively constrain Cost of Personnel exposure**
- Avoid long-term, high-volume labor assignments
- Reserve for discrete, high-value, time-bound roles

### 6.2 Small / Specialized Subcontractors
- Use for scalable execution
- Assign high-volume or repeatable work
- Support margin stabilization and FAR compliance

**Teaming is a design variable, not a fixed constraint.**

### 6.3 OCI/Teaming Conflict Protocol [NEW]

Before finalizing any teaming arrangement, validate:
1. No Organizational Conflict of Interest (OCI) with evaluation or advisory roles
2. No exclusivity agreements with competing primes
3. No active non-compete clauses covering the scope
4. No incumbent "poison pill" clauses

**If conflict detected → Flag for legal/contracts review before proceeding.**

## SECTION 10: MANDATORY WRITING RULES

### 10.1 Feature → Benefit → Proof (Shipley Rule)

Never state a feature without all three elements.

**Required structure:**
> "rockITdata leverages [FEATURE] to deliver [MEASURABLE BENEFIT], validated by [PROOF]."

**Banned phrases:**
- ~~"We understand"~~
- ~~"We believe"~~
- ~~"Best-in-class"~~
- ~~"World-class"~~
- ~~"State-of-the-art"~~
- ~~"Strive to"~~
- ~~"Next-generation"~~

**If it cannot be proven, it does not exist.**

### 10.2 Government Evaluator Risk Rule

#### Continuity Guarantee
Every AI, automation, or modernization claim must include explicit risk mitigation.

**Required pattern:**
> "To ensure continuity of current operations, rockITdata will operate legacy processes in parallel with the modernized solution during a defined transition period, ensuring zero service disruption."

#### Explainable AI (Mandatory)
AI must never be described as autonomous or opaque.

**Required pattern:**
> "All AI-assisted outputs are explainable, auditable, and traceable, with documented rationale aligned to governing policy and regulation."

### 10.3 Financial & FAR Discipline Rule

- FAR 52.219-14 compliance is based on Cost of Personnel, not hours
- Large subcontractors increase compliance and margin risk
- **Staffing rules:**
  - Assign high-volume, repeatable tasks to rockITdata and small businesses
  - Reserve large subs for discrete, high-value, time-bound roles
  - Redesign staffing plans that threaten compliance or margin

**Do not justify bad labor mixes—correct them.**
