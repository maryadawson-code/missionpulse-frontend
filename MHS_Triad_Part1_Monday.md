## The Big Picture: We're the "Department of War" Now

[IMAGE: Secretary of War plaque]

On September 5, 2025, President Trump signed Executive Order 14347, authorizing the Pentagon to use "Department of War" as a secondary title. Defense.gov now redirects to war.gov. The signs outside Secretary Hegseth's office read "Secretary of War." This isn't just optics. It's doctrine.

Here's the friction nobody's talking about: the "Department of War Restoration Act" is sitting in the House Armed Services Committee, and until it passes, every official contract still has to say "Department of Defense" to be valid. The disconnect between the political name (War) and the legal name (Defense) is already causing headaches for contracting officers. It tells you everything about how this administration operates: move fast, deal with the paperwork later.

For the MHS, this means the metric of success is no longer "patient satisfaction scores" or "beneficiary access to primary care." It's **return-to-duty rates** and **survivability of the force**.

The benefit mission isn't going away. But it's about to take a back seat to combat readiness in ways we haven't seen since the Cold War.

The **"Golden Dome Act" provisions** (sponsored by Sen. Sullivan) were incorporated into the FY2026 NDAA, authorizing multi-year procurement for 10 missile types and codifying the layered homeland missile defense system. A huge chunk of the $291 billion O&M authorization (where healthcare lives) is getting squeezed by missile defense procurement. If your tech doesn't directly support survivability, it's on the chopping block.

## The Audit Mandate: Why Everything Has to Work Now

Here's the context nobody's talking about: **DoD must achieve a clean audit opinion by December 31, 2028.** That's not a goal. It's a congressional mandate in the NDAA FY2024. And as of FY2024, DoD remains the only major federal agency that has never received a clean audit opinion on its financial statements.

The Marine Corps broke through in FY2023, becoming the first military service to achieve a clean opinion. But the rest of DoD, including DHA, is still working through disclaimers.

Why does this matter for the Triad? Because **Section 1002 of the FY2026 NDAA** gives this mandate real teeth: DoD now has authority to **fire or demote Senior Executive Service (SES) employees** for poor performance related to audit failures. That's the stick Bass is holding. If he can't make MHS GENESIS audit-compliant, his SES leadership gets fired.

For DHA specifically, this means:

**MHS GENESIS isn't optional.** The EHR modernization isn't just about patient care. It's about getting DHA's $56 billion in annual spending onto auditable systems.

**Telehealth platform consolidation is coming.** You can't pass an audit with dozens of shadow IT telehealth systems scattered across 721 MTFs.

**Wearables and RPM require data architecture.** The reason the Oura contract died wasn't just WHOOP's protest. It's that DHA couldn't demonstrate how wearables data flows into auditable health records.

The Triad inherits a system that's simultaneously trying to transform for great power conflict AND pass a financial audit for the first time in history. Those two goals don't always align, and the tension will define what actually gets funded versus what gets cut.

## The Political Architect: Keith Bass

**Confirmed:** January 5, 2026 | **Vote:** 50-35 | **Start Date:** January 12, 2026

Keith Bass is the hatchet man. He's here to cut costs, enforce compliance, and take the political heat so the operators can build the combat medical force.

**The Background:**

Bass is a retired Navy Commander with a background in rehabilitation counseling and healthcare administration. He's held some of the most sensitive medical leadership positions in government: Director of the White House Medical Unit under three administrations, Director of the CIA's Office of Medical Services, and most recently, Director of the West Texas VA Health Care System.

But here's what most people miss: between his CIA departure and the VA appointment, Bass spent 15 months in the private sector as **Senior Vice President of Business and Clinical Development at GlobalMed**, one of only two telemedicine vendors with deep operational medicine credentials in DoD. The other is Medweb, and together these two companies dominate the deployed telehealth landscape. Bass knows exactly how the government buys telehealth, because he was on the other side of that transaction.

**The Controversy:**

Senator Elizabeth Warren went after Bass hard during his confirmation, citing a DoD Inspector General report that found "severe and systemic problems" in the White House pharmacy during his tenure. The report documented controlled substances (Ambien, Provigil) being dispensed without proper prescriptions or patient verification.

Warren's letter was brutal: "At best, you were oblivious to the wide distribution of sedatives and stimulants by WHMU's pharmacy operations and, in the worst case, you were complicit."

Bass also faced questions about his time at CIA, where sources told SpyTalk he was "pushed out" after fumbling the agency's response to Havana Syndrome cases.

**The Redemption:**

Despite all that, Bass got confirmed with crossover votes from Democratic Senators Fetterman, Gallego, Kaine, Reed, Rosen, and Shaheen, plus Independent Senator King. Republican Susan Collins was the lone GOP vote against. Why did Democrats cross over? Because at the West Texas VA, Bass delivered results.

Bass went all-in on telehealth and remote patient monitoring. The VA-wide Home Telehealth program he championed showed impressive numbers: 41% reduction in hospital admissions, 70% reduction in bed-days of care for enrolled veterans. He proved he could run a lean, tech-forward healthcare operation in a resource-constrained environment.

**What to Watch:**

Bass will likely implement the strictest pharmacy controls in DoD history (he can't afford another scandal). But more importantly, he's going to scale the telehealth model across the entire MHS.

His GlobalMed background is a double-edged sword. Bass knows the platform cold, but that's also a massive ethics trap. Expect rigorous scrutiny on any sole-source awards. Competitors like Amwell and Teladoc are watching his every move for a reason to file a bid protest.

Either way, expect aggressive pushes to move retiree and dependent care onto virtual platforms, freeing up brick-and-mortar capacity for active duty readiness training.

## The Silicon Valley Disruptor: Emil Michael

**Status:** USD Research & Engineering since May 2025

Emil Michael is the Pentagon's CTO, and he's operating like he's still in a Series B startup, except his product is American military dominance.

**The AI Push:**

On December 9, Michael launched GenAI.mil, putting Google's Gemini for Government on every DoD desktop. His quote: "There is no prize for second place in the global race for AI dominance. AI is America's next Manifest Destiny."

This isn't a pilot program. This is 3 million users, right now, at every classification level. Michael wants AI embedded in "the daily battle rhythm" of every warfighter.

But GenAI.mil isn't the only game in town. **AskSage**, the AI assistant developed by Sage Technologies for defense and intelligence communities, is already deployed across DoD with FedRAMP High authorization. While GenAI.mil brings consumer-grade AI to the unclassified network, AskSage has been quietly building the muscle memory for AI adoption at the SECRET and above levels.

For DHA, this dual-track AI deployment matters. The agency identified **120+ active AI projects** in January 2024 spanning machine learning for mental health, radiology imaging, genomics, and clinical documentation. A Fall 2024 red teaming exercise tested three major LLMs for clinical note summarization, generating 800+ findings on biases and vulnerabilities. The question isn't whether AI comes to military medicine. It's which platforms get embedded in clinical workflows first.

**The University War:**

Michael is waging war on what he sees as bloated academic overhead. In May, Secretary Hegseth signed a memo capping indirect cost reimbursements for university research at 15%, down from negotiated rates that often exceeded 50%.

The Association of American Universities sued immediately. They got a temporary restraining order in June, a preliminary injunction in July, and on October 15, Judge Brian Murphy vacated the entire policy as "arbitrary and capricious."

But Michael isn't backing down. The government filed an appeal in December. And if he can't cap costs through policy, he'll shift funding away from universities entirely, toward commercial vendors and government labs.

**The China Decoupling:**

On January 8, Michael announced new actions to protect DoD-funded research from "malign foreign influence." **Section 223 of the FY2026 NDAA** prohibits funding to entities on the 1260H list (Chinese military companies) and codifies the "Fundamental Research Risk Review Repository" as a **statutory requirement**, not just policy.

This is the new "CMMC for Research." Ignore it at your peril.

If you have a university research partner in your supply chain, you need to check the Risk Review Repository immediately. If your academic partner has a lab funded by a "foreign entity of concern," your DoD funding could be paused overnight. For anyone in the medical research enterprise using Chinese biotech equipment (BGI sequencers, for example), this is a wake-up call.

## The Operational Commander: Admiral Darin Via

**Status:** Navy Surgeon General; Expected DHA Director (February 2026)

Admiral Via is the uniformed operator who will translate all of this into capability at the tip of the spear.

**The Doctrine:**

Via coined the term that defines his approach: the "Human Weapon System."

His quote from December 2023: "We directly support warfighter readiness by optimizing the most important weapon system in our arsenal, the human weapon system that fights and wins our nation's wars."

This isn't healthcare. This is **Human Performance Optimization**. Cognitive enhancement. Nutritional optimization. Sleep resilience. Via wants athletic trainers and strength coaches embedded in every infantry battalion, treating medics as "maintenance crews for the human platform."

[IMAGE: Human Weapon System soldier with biometric HUD]

> **"We directly support warfighter readiness by optimizing the most important weapon system in our arsenal, the human weapon system."** — Admiral Darin Via, December 2023

**The Bureaucratic Innovation:**

Via was the first medical flag officer to stand up OPNAV N44, making Navy Medicine a "Resource Sponsor" with a seat at the budget table alongside the people who buy ships and missiles. He institutionalized the idea that medical capability is a "kill chain" enabler, not overhead.

He'll bring that same bureaucratic jujitsu to DHA.

**The Pacific Problem:**

Via's strategic nightmare is the "tyranny of distance" in a war with China. Air superiority isn't guaranteed. The "Golden Hour" evacuation to a major hospital is a fantasy.

His solution: the Role 2E Expeditionary Medical Unit. Light, mobile surgical teams on high-speed vessels that can hold casualties for 96 hours when evacuation is blocked. And critically, data systems that work **offline**, because in a contested environment, the satellite link is going down.

[IMAGE: Role 2E EPF vessel]

This is exactly why DARPA is pouring money into programs like MASH and GOLDEVAC: autonomous systems that can keep casualties alive for 48+ hours without continuous medic intervention. Via's operational concept and DARPA's research portfolio are converging on the same requirement: extend survivability when the golden hour becomes the golden day.

## How the Triad Works Together

Bass, Michael, and Via aren't operating in silos. They're an interlocking system.

**Bass + Via (The Efficiency-Readiness Loop):**
Bass cuts costs in the rear by pushing beneficiary care to virtual platforms and privatized networks. Via uses the savings (and the freed-up uniformed personnel) to build the expeditionary medical force for the Pacific. The H2F model proves the ROI: $3 million per brigade delivers 35% injury reduction. Scale that math, and the business case writes itself.

**Michael + Bass (The Secure Supply Chain):**
Michael identifies the Chinese biotech threat. Bass enforces the blacklist across TRICARE providers and research partners. Result: "genomic sovereignty" for military medical data. The DARPA programs feeding the pipeline (MASH, GOLDEVAC, ITM) need domestic manufacturing and secure data architectures. Michael's China decoupling accelerates that transition.

**Michael + Via (The Autonomous Front):**
Via defines the operational gap: can't evacuate casualties from the First Island Chain with manned helicopters. Michael bypasses traditional acquisition to buy drones for medical resupply. DARPA's Triage Challenge winners (Battelle's DART system) show the way. Capability fielded in 18-24 months instead of 10 years.

If they coordinate (and that's a big if), the technology stack for the Human Weapon System doctrine could be operational by 2028.
