## SECTION 1: EVALUATION METHODOLOGY ANALYSIS

### DHA Uses Best Value Continuum (NOT LPTA)

**Source:** Attachment 3, Addendum to 52.212-2, Page 1

> "The basis for award for this acquisition will be the **Best Value Continuum**, considering a tradeoff between technical approach, past performance and price to determine the most advantageous offer to the Government. **The Government may award to other than the lowest-priced Offeror** or other than the highest technically rated Offeror if the decision is determined to be in the best interest of the Government."

**What This Means:**
- **NOT Lowest Price Technically Acceptable (LPTA)** = Technical superiority CAN justify higher price
- **Qualitative tradeoff** = No numeric scoring formulas; evaluators use judgment
- **"May award to other than lowest price"** = rockITdata's $1.84M is competitive if technical rating is high

---

**Factor 1: Technical Capability (MOST IMPORTANT)**

- **Rating Scale:** Outstanding / Good / Acceptable / Marginal / Unacceptable
- **Note:** If ANY subfactor receives "Unacceptable," entire technical rating becomes "Unacceptable"

**Factor 2: Past Performance**
- **Assessment:** Substantial Confidence / Satisfactory Confidence / Limited Confidence / No Confidence / Unknown Confidence
- **Criteria:** Recency (last 3 years), Relevancy (similar scope), Quality (performance record)
- **rockITdata Status:** LMI subcontractor's "Exceptional" Army ADAP rating provides "Substantial Confidence"
