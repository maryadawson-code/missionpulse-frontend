## The Audit Mandate: Why Everything Has to Work Now

Here's the context nobody's talking about: **DoD must achieve a clean audit opinion by December 31, 2028.** That's not a goal—it's a congressional mandate in the NDAA FY2024. And as of FY2024, DoD remains the only major federal agency that has never received a clean audit opinion on its financial statements.

The Marine Corps broke through in FY2023, becoming the first military service to achieve a clean opinion. But the rest of DoD—including DHA—is still working through disclaimers.

Why does this matter for the Triad? Because **Section 1002 of the FY2026 NDAA** gives this mandate real teeth: DoD now has authority to **fire or demote Senior Executive Service (SES) employees** for poor performance related to audit failures. That's the stick Bass is holding. If he can't make MHS GENESIS audit-compliant, his SES leadership gets fired. This is why the Oura deal—with its non-auditable data architecture—was toxic from the start.

The GAO's September 2025 report identified 89 outdated information systems DoD is retiring to meet audit requirements—projected to save $760 million annually through FY2029.

For DHA specifically, this means:

**MHS GENESIS isn't optional.** The EHR modernization isn't just about patient care—it's about getting DHA's $56 billion in annual spending onto auditable systems. Oracle's Clinical AI Agent deployment through DHMSM? That's not just reducing clinician burnout—it's creating structured, traceable documentation that auditors can actually verify.

**Telehealth platform consolidation is coming.** You can't pass an audit with dozens of shadow IT telehealth systems scattered across 721 MTFs. Bass and Via will have to rationalize the platform landscape—and anything that doesn't integrate with MHS GENESIS is going to have a hard time surviving.

**Wearables and RPM require data architecture.** The reason the Oura contract died wasn't just WHOOP's protest—it's that DHA couldn't justify a $96 million sole-source deal when they can't demonstrate the data governance infrastructure to make the investment auditable. Until DHA can show how wearables data flows into auditable health records, enterprise wearables programs are going to stall.

The Triad inherits a system that's simultaneously trying to transform for great power conflict AND pass a financial audit for the first time in history. Those two goals don't always align—and the tension will define what actually gets funded versus what gets cut.

## The Research Pipeline: DARPA, H2F, and the Technology Via Needs

Via's "Human Weapon System" doctrine isn't just philosophy—it requires technology that's currently in development across DARPA, the services, and the research enterprise. Understanding this pipeline tells you where the MHS is headed and who's positioned to win.

**DARPA's Combat Medicine Portfolio:**

The Biological Technologies Office is building the autonomous systems Via needs for contested environments. The headline program is **MASH (Medics Autonomously Stopping Hemorrhage)**—$32.4 million announced September 2025 to develop robotic systems that autonomously detect and stop internal bleeding. Non-compressible torso hemorrhage kills 25% of potentially survivable combat casualties. MASH aims to make intervention as simple as deploying an AED.

**GOLDEVAC** complements this with automated resuscitation through a single intravascular catheter—oxygenation, medication, fluid resuscitation for up to 48 hours without continuous medic intervention. Add in **SHIELD** (circulating treatments that prevent wound infection for a week) and **BEST** (autonomous smart bandages), and you see the vision: keep casualties alive long enough for evacuation that might be days away, not hours.

On the AI side, DARPA's **In the Moment (ITM)** program has been training algorithms for triage decision-making for four years. The breakthrough: they're optimizing for alignment with experienced military medical personnel's judgment, not just clinical outcomes. Phase 2 extends to mass casualty scenarios. The **DARPA Triage Challenge** just crowned winners—Battelle's DART system uses drone-mounted sensors to autonomously identify casualties in crash sites and ambush scenarios.

For Human Performance Optimization, watch **AWARE** (photoswitchable drugs for alertness without stimulant side effects), **RESTORE** (neuromodulation to maintain cognitive performance on three hours of sleep), and **SNAP** ($17.7 million for handheld sensors assessing warfighter readiness through biomarkers).

**The H2F Model—And Why It Matters:**

The Army's **Holistic Health and Fitness (H2F)** program is the proof-of-concept for Via's doctrine. Now deployed to 71 brigades—up from 50 in October 2024—with full Army implementation targeted by 2032 (236 brigades). The numbers are striking:

- **35% reduction** in injury volume
- **37% fewer suicides** versus 37% *increase* in non-H2F units
- **23% higher** ACFT pass rate
- **27% more** soldiers qualifying expert in rifle marksmanship

Each H2F Performance Team includes 20-22 contractors: strength coaches, dietitians, physical therapists, cognitive performance specialists. Cost is $3 million per brigade to field, $2.5 million annual sustainment. The Army's Warrior Performance Platform just got DoD IL4 authorization—2,300 soldiers registered, 70,000 workout sets tracked, 150,000 wearable data points captured.

**The Gap: Navy and Air Force Lag Behind**

Here's the problem for Via: outside special operations, neither the Navy nor Air Force has anything comparable to H2F. The Navy's NOFFS provides resources but no embedded teams. Only Naval Special Warfare has dedicated human performance staff. GAO found only 14% of Navy officers get adequate sleep—and the Navy needs a 15-year plan just to achieve 100% crew manning.

The Air Force's Comprehensive Airman Fitness is a framework, not a program. Only AFSPECWAR has embedded teams.

The exception? The **Space Force**, which deployed Guardian Resilience Teams to all bases with certified strength coaches, mental health providers, and physical therapists. Their September 2025 Human Performance and Readiness Manual is the closest service equivalent to Army H2F.

If Via becomes DHA Director, expect him to push Army H2F principles across the joint force—and the data foundation to support it becomes critical.

Here's the legislative hook that makes this possible: the FY2026 NDAA includes **"SPEED Act" language** mandating portfolio-based acquisition. This allows Via to buy "Human Performance" as a **portfolio capability** rather than running 50 separate contracts for dietitians, wearables, and gyms. That legal change is what enables the H2F expansion we're predicting.

But the August 2025 contract extension came with cuts: behavioral health automation and remote care programs were excluded due to DoD budget restrictions. Amwell's SEC filings acknowledge they may not maintain the DHA relationship beyond July 2026 under comparable terms.

**GlobalMed** holds the DHA Med COI Authority to Operate—renewed in December 2025—making them the only synchronous clinical telehealth platform authorized across all 721 military treatment facilities. Their hardware-integrated approach (40+ medical devices, transportable exam stations) enables clinical-grade remote examinations. They power telehealth for the White House Medical Unit, have been in the VA since 2008, and recently hired Major General Michael Talley as Senior Advisor. Bass spent 15 months there as SVP. They know MTF operations cold.

**Medweb** owns the deployed telehealth mission. Their $52.8 million Deployed Tele-Radiology System contract provides PACS and tele-radiology services to over 40 Navy vessels and all six geographic Combatant Commands. Their platforms are on every deployed Navy ship—Role 2 and Role 3 facilities—and throughout CENTCOM and INDOPACOM. They've built something unique: a DICOM data scrubber that handles security challenges of moving medical imaging across classification levels. They're the bridge for Joint Longitudinal Viewer (JLV) integration coming out of Chicago. And their store-and-forward dermatology application is the platform of choice for the American Academy of Dermatology's Access Derm program.

**VSee** holds FedRAMP High authorization from HHS (granted October 2025) and won a $6 million first-year federal contract in January 2025. They appear on Humana Military's recommended provider list but lack direct DHA platform contracting—yet. Watch this space.

**Outpatient App** is the one to watch. This small business just secured their **SBIR Phase III**, setting them up for the Tradewinds marketplace—the DoD's streamlined pathway for scaling proven commercial solutions across the enterprise. Their Virtual Health Management (VHM) System handles provider-to-provider communication, consult coordination, and on-call scheduling—the operational plumbing that enterprise platforms often miss. They've co-developed medical readiness coordination technology with Holloman AFB and JBSA, automating deployment medical clearance workflows that currently require searching five databases and two networks. They'll be at DHITS 2025 in Nashville presenting their Readiness Scheduling System (RISS).

Here's what matters: they solve a real problem and have strong backing from service leadership. That's the combination that gets you from pilot to production. Small, nimble, solving actual MTF pain points with Air Force Medical buy-in—exactly the kind of company that either gets absorbed into a larger platform play, becomes a DHMSM subcontractor, or scales through Tradewinds to become the next enterprise standard.

**Teladoc** operates as a TRICARE network provider through regional managed care contractors rather than holding a direct DHA platform contract. Beneficiaries access Teladoc's urgent care, behavioral health, and dermatology services through Humana Military (East) and TriWest (West).

**Tele-Critical Care:**

The **Joint Tele-Critical Care Network (JTCCN)** provides 24/7 remote intensivist coverage using Philips eICU technology—addressing a critical shortage of intensivists across garrison ICUs. The network operates from four hubs: Naval Medical Center San Diego (Virtual Medical Operations Center), Brooke Army Medical Center, Madigan Army Medical Center, and Walter Reed. They support **18+ satellite MTFs and 120+ ICU beds** globally, including OCONUS locations in Guam, Japan, Spain, Djibouti, and Diego Garcia.

DHA is expanding this network—and it's where Bass's governance experience and Via's operational focus intersect. The tele-critical care infrastructure built for beneficiaries becomes the backbone for Role 2E remote surgical consultation in contested environments.

**The T-5 Managed Care Structure:**

The $136 billion T-5 contracts that went live January 1, 2025 reshape regional telehealth delivery:

- **Humana Military (East):** $70.9 billion ceiling, ~4.6 million beneficiaries
- **TriWest (West):** $65.1 billion ceiling, ~5 million beneficiaries (absorbed six states from East Region)

Virtual health network partners include Doctor on Demand, Teladoc, Telemynd, and Talkspace. Specialty telehealth includes SimpliFed (lactation), Expressable (speech therapy), and Birches Health (addiction).

**What's Missing—and What's Coming:**

**Remote Patient Monitoring (RPM)** represents the biggest gap. DHA conducted a Reverse Industry Day in December 2022 soliciting commercial RPM capabilities. No enterprise vendor contract has been awarded—the program remains in market research.

**The Oura wearables deal is dead.** The $96 million sole-source contract to Ouraring Inc. announced in October 2024 collapsed after WHOOP filed two GAO protests arguing the "ring-based form factor" requirement unfairly excluded 97% of the commercial wearables market. DHA initially withdrew the contract, then reissued a solicitation that still specified rings—and acknowledged to lawmakers that Oura was the only ring company cleared for DoD secure facilities. WHOOP filed a second protest in January 2025. In March, DHA cancelled the entire acquisition, telling GAO it was "no longer required" and "no longer considered a priority."

But the protest was just the trigger. The real story? DHA realized it couldn't audit the data.

The deal didn't die because of form factor politics—it died because DHA couldn't demonstrate how wearable data would flow securely into auditable health records. Rep. Morgan Luttrell (R-TX) called out the "preference for relying on a single provider" as limiting innovation, but the deeper issue is data governance. The next wearables solicitation won't just ask for a ring or a strap—it will ask for an **Audit-Ready Data Architecture**. If you can't show how your wearable data flows into MHS GENESIS with full traceability, don't bother bidding.

The service-level pilots (DIU studies, Air Force First Sergeant Academy, USS Essex) will continue generating proof points. But enterprise DHA procurement is off the table until the audit infrastructure catches up.

**Behavioral health digital therapeutics** took a hit when the Amwell contract extension excluded these programs. No enterprise digital mental health platform comparable to Lyra Health, Spring Health, or Headspace is deployed.

**Ambient clinical intelligence** is already here. DHA is scaling **Oracle's Clinical AI Agent** through an active DHMSM task order, starting with ambient listening capabilities. This automates clinical documentation by listening to patient encounters and generating structured notes directly into MHS GENESIS—no separate integration required. Clinicians at deployed sites are already seeing 2+ hours per day returned to patient care. This is exactly the kind of EHR-native capability we argued for in the Digital First pitch: tools that integrate appropriately rather than bolting on from the outside.

**The Digital Front Door is dead.** The December 2024 prototype awards to BDR, Bluestaq, Clearstep, and Ernst & Young will not move forward to production. Budget pressures and competing priorities killed what could have been DHA's first comprehensive patient-facing digital platform. The extensible ecosystem we pitched to Crosland—AI-powered triage, care navigation, mobile-native design—remains unrealized at the enterprise level. This is a significant loss for beneficiary experience, but it tells you where the Triad's priorities lie: operational readiness over patient convenience.

**The Vendor Landscape:**

GlobalMed and Medweb remain complementary and competitive. GlobalMed dominates the garrison MTF environment; Medweb owns the deployed operational space.
